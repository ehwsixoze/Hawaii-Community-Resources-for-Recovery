Federal Recovery Housing Program

Transitional Housing

Federal program providing stable, transitional housing for individuals in recovery from substance use disorders. Assistance is limited to 2 years or until permanent housing is secured, whichever comes first.

📞

Contact Information

Program Info: HUD.gov/recovery-housing

✓

Eligibility Requirements

  • In recovery from substance use disorder
  • Meet state eligibility criteria
  • Income and housing status requirements vary by program
  • Priority for those exiting treatment

Financial Assistance

Government programs providing temporary financial support during recovery transition

💰

Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF/TAONF)

Cash Assistance

Provides monthly cash benefits to families for basic needs including food, clothing, and shelter. TANF serves U.S. citizens, while TAONF is Hawaii's state program for non-citizens. Includes substance abuse treatment requirements when needed.

📞

Contact Information

Website: humanservices.hawaii.gov/bessd/tanf/
Apply Online: pais-benefits.dhs.hawaii.gov
Phone: 1-855-643-1643
Processing: Centers statewide
✓

Eligibility Requirements

  • Hawaii resident with children under 18
  • Gross income below 185% Federal Poverty Level
  • Must participate in First-To-Work Program
  • Seeking substance abuse treatment if determined necessary
  • U.S. citizenship (TANF) or legal status (TAONF)
Families with Children
Single Parents
Two-Parent Families
Apply Online Call for Help
